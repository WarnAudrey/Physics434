Part C¶

If you have a trials factor of 10000, the signal would have to be 1.31 times brighter in order to be considered a significant discovery. The sensitivity penalty for a trials factor of 10k is so low in these particular distributions because the probability of the background producing a signal greater than 1 was already so low in the initial distribution, so when the number of trials was increased, it had little to no effect. We can see the probability of seeing a signal of around 5 in the initial distribution was around 10e-9, so the probability of seeing that signal in the distribution after 10000 trials was 10e-5. As a result, we can see the change in probability is incredible tiny when comparing 1 trial to 10000 trials, and so the sensitivity penalty of the trial factory is not very severe for these distributions.

Part D As you increase the magnitude of the trials factor by 10, the 5 sigma threshold increases by between .3-.4 signal strength each time i.e. if we increase the trials factor from 10,000 to 100,000, the 5-sigma signal value goes from 6.55 to 6.89. So the signal needs to increase in strength by roughly .3 to .4 for each increase in trial factor by a factor of 10.¶
